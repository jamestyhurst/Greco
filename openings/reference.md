# Greco opening-theory reference

Greco loads every `.md` file in this `openings/` folder and includes it in the
analysis prompt as authoritative reference for opening **names** and
**variations**. Add your own notes or convert opening PDFs to text/markdown and
drop them here to deepen Greco's opening knowledge.

**Naming principle:** identify the opening by the player's FIRST move and actual
move order, not by a structure it later transposes into. Name the opening the
player chose; mention transpositions as secondary.

---

## 1.e4 openings (by Black's reply)

- **1...e5** — Open Game / Double King's Pawn. Leads to Ruy Lopez (3.Bb5), Italian (3.Bc4), Scotch (3.d4), Petroff (2...Nf6), King's Gambit (2.f4), Vienna (2.Nc3).
- **1...c5** — Sicilian Defence. Najdorf (5...a6), Dragon (...g6), Scheveningen (...e6/...d6), Sveshnikov (...e5), Accelerated Dragon, Taimanov, Classical.
- **1...e6** — French Defence. Advance (3.e5), Tarrasch (3.Nd2), Winawer (3.Nc3 Bb4), Classical (3.Nc3 Nf6), Exchange (3.exd5).
- **1...c6** — Caro-Kann Defence. Advance, Classical (4...Bf5), Two Knights, Exchange, Panov-Botvinnik.
- **1...d5** — Scandinavian / Centre-Counter Defence. (2.exd5 Qxd5 or 2...Nf6.)
- **1...Nf6** — **Alekhine's Defence.** Black provokes the e-pawn forward.
  - **2.e5 Nd5** is the main line: Modern/Main (3.d4 d6 4.Nf3), Four Pawns Attack (4.c4 Nb6 5.f4), Exchange (4.c4 Nb6 5.exd6).
  - **2.Nc3** (declining) often transposes; if Black answers **2...d5**, this is the **Scandinavian Variation of Alekhine's Defence** (Black strikes the centre; after 3.e5 the play takes on a French-Advance character, but the opening is still Alekhine's by move order).
- **1...d6** — Pirc Defence (with ...g6, ...Bg7); **1...g6** — Modern Defence.
- **1...Nc6** — Nimzowitsch Defence.

## 1.d4 openings

- **1...Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.Nc3 Bb4** — Nimzo-Indian. **3...d5** — Queen's Gambit Declined. **2...g6 with ...d6/...e5** — King's Indian Defence. **2...g6 with ...d5** — Grünfeld. **2...e6 3.Nf3 b6** — Queen's Indian.
- **1...d5 2.c4** — Queen's Gambit: Accepted (2...dxc4), Declined (2...e6), Slav (2...c6).
- **1...f5** — Dutch Defence.

## Other first moves

- **1.Nf3** — Réti / King's Indian Attack setups. **1.c4** — English Opening. **1.g3** — Hypermodern/KIA.

## Notes on "book" judgement

A move is a **book move** when it matches established theory for the opening
actually being played (use the lines above and your own opening knowledge).
Book moves should be acknowledged as theory, never flagged as "inaccuracies"
merely because an engine has a small numerical preference. Reserve criticism for
genuine departures that lose material or walk into a concrete tactic.
